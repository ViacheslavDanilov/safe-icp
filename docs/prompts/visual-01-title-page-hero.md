# Visual 01: Title Page Hero

Purpose:

- visual for the title slide
- communicate non-invasive optical sensing and brain monitoring

Recommended defaults:

- aspect ratio `1:1`
- photoreal / high realism
- realistic scientific tone
- no text, no logos, no watermark

This visual should feel real rather than cinematic. Below are three alternative prompt directions.

## Variant A: Clinical-Documentary

```text
Create a realistic academic-presentation visual showing non-invasive optical intracranial pressure monitoring in a believable clinical-research setting. Show a human subject or patient seated or lying down, with a compact optical probe placed externally on the forehead. The device should look like real optical medical instrumentation, not futuristic and not cinematic. The scene should resemble a genuine hospital research room or medical technology lab, with realistic lighting, realistic skin tones, realistic anatomy, and subtle visible instrumentation. The key idea is that brain-related physiological information is being measured from outside the skull using light. Keep the visual grounded, clean, and scientifically credible. Avoid dramatic visual effects; if optical sensing is shown, it should be extremely subtle and physically plausible. No surgery, no gore, no text, no logos, no holographic overlays, no science-fiction aesthetics.
```

## Variant B: Device-Focused

```text
Create a realistic scientific visual for a seminar presentation focused on a compact non-invasive optical brain-monitoring setup. Show the forehead probe and the connected optical instrumentation clearly, with a human subject wearing the probe in a believable clinical or research environment. Emphasize that the sensing happens externally on the forehead, without any invasive access. The device should look practical, real, and medically credible, as if photographed during a university-hospital research study. Lighting should be natural and restrained, with clear details of the probe, cables, and surrounding equipment. Avoid cinematic drama and avoid speculative visual effects. No surgery, no gore, no text, no logos, no futuristic styling.
```

## Variant C: Research-Lab

```text
Create a realistic university-lab visual showing a non-invasive optical monitoring experiment related to intracranial pressure estimation. Show a participant or patient with a forehead-mounted optical probe in a clean clinical-research environment, with researchers’ equipment visible in the background. The setup should look like genuine translational medical research rather than a marketing render. The image should communicate that brain-related physiological information is being measured safely from outside the skull. Keep anatomy and instrumentation realistic, use restrained lighting, and avoid cinematic color grading or science-fiction effects. No surgery, no gore, no text, no logos, no artificial HUD elements.
```

## Variant D: Abstract Optical Signal Field

```text
Create an abstract scientific visual for the title page of a presentation about non-invasive brain-pressure monitoring. Do not show a full person or a literal hospital scene. Instead, depict a dark, elegant field of subtle optical textures, speckle-like signal patterns, flowing wave structures, and a restrained circular sensing focal point that implies forehead-based light measurement. The composition should feel intelligent, precise, and research-driven rather than cinematic or futuristic. Use a dark background with controlled warm amber and cool teal accents, strong negative space, and clean geometry. The image should suggest brain-related physiological signal extraction through light without becoming a generic technology wallpaper. No text, no logos, no obvious medical icons, no holographic HUD, no glowing sci-fi spectacle.
```

## Variant E: Minimal Probe-And-Signal Concept

```text
Create a minimal semi-abstract title-page visual for a presentation about non-invasive optical intracranial pressure estimation. Show only a small part of the real-world setup: a compact optical probe touching the forehead region or hovering very close to it, with the rest of the composition fading into darkness. Surround the probe area with subtle light diffusion, fine speckle-inspired texture, and calm flowing signal structures that imply physiological measurement. Keep the anatomy partial and understated, not fully literal, so the image feels more like an elegant scientific concept than a documentary scene. The style should be clean, restrained, dark, and premium, suitable for a university seminar title slide. No surgery, no gore, no text, no logos, no consumer-tech look, no flashy science-fiction overlays.
```
