# Visual 02: Invasive ICP Monitoring Procedure

Purpose:

- show the current invasive clinical approach on its own
- support the “why ICP matters / why current measurement is limiting” part of the talk

Recommended defaults:

- aspect ratio `1:1`
- photoreal / high realism
- dark editorial scientific tone
- no text, no logos, no watermark

Prompt:

```text
Create a medically credible academic-presentation visual showing invasive intracranial pressure monitoring in a realistic neurosurgical context. Show a clinician preparing or placing an intracranial pressure sensor through a burr-hole procedure, with sterile draping, precise surgical setup, hospital lighting, and appropriate equipment. The scene should communicate that this is a serious invasive hospital procedure, but it must remain clean and presentation-safe: no visible blood, no graphic trauma, no horror elements. The mood should be clinical, restrained, and scientifically credible. Dark editorial color treatment is preferred, with realistic anatomy and realistic medical equipment. No text, no labels, no logos.
```
